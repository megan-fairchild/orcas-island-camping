🏕️ Camp Prep Checklist

Today, June 16. Trip starts tomorrow at 8:45 AM from Snohomish.

1. Gear Inspection Start here

Pull everything out. Check for damage, mildew, and missing parts before you pack.
  • Tent: set up in yard, check poles, stakes, and rainfly for tears or rust
  • Air mattress: inflate fully, leave 15 min to check for leaks
  • Air mattress pump: verify it works, check batteries or charge
  • Sleeping bags (3): unroll, check zippers, shake out debris
  • Camp stove: test ignition, check fuel level
  • Pie irons (2): clean, check hinge
  • Cast iron pan: check for rust, re-season if needed
  • Cooler: wash interior, check seal/latch
  • Lantern and headlamps (3): test batteries, pack extras
  • Camp chairs (3): check fabric for tears, test fold mechanisms
  • CamelBak backpack: rinse bladder, check for mold, dry
  • Life jacket (son): check buckles and fit
  • Hammock: untangle, confirm no rips
  • Bluetooth speaker: charge fully
  • Dog leashes, harnesses, and tie-outs: check clips and wear

2. Shopping Trip Late morning

Buy hammock straps (MalloMe XL) if not already ordered. Check the full grocery list on the meals page.
  • Hammock tree straps (BUY: MalloMe XL)
  • Hot dogs + buns
  • Bacon (1 pack)
  • 2 cans tuna
  • Shrimp or chicken (depending on swap choice)
  • Pancake mix (just-add-water)
  • Maple syrup
  • Eggs (8), butter
  • Sandwich bread (2 loaves)
  • Peanut butter, honey
  • Chips (2-3 bags)
  • Pasta salad (buy pre-made or ingredients to make)
  • Produce: grapes, baby carrots, snap peas, bananas, fruit
  • Pie iron fillings: marinara, mozzarella, turkey pepperoni, sliced ham, Nutella
  • S'mores: marshmallows, graham crackers, chocolate bars
  • Hot cocoa packets, juice boxes
  • Ketchup, mustard, relish
  • Dog food (measured per day), dog treats, poop bags
  • Ice for cooler
  • Firewood (or plan to buy at park)
  • Extra batteries
  • Bug spray, sunscreen SPF 30+

3. Food Prep Afternoon

These items travel better if made today. Store in cooler tomorrow morning.
  • Make tuna salad (for Day 2 lunch)
  • Make pasta salad (for Day 1 and Day 2 dinners)
  • Prep scrambled egg mixture in a sealed container (for Day 2 breakfast)
  • Make no-bake cookies (snack for all days)
  • Portion trail mix into bags
  • Wash and cut veggies: carrots, snap peas
  • Measure dog food into per-day bags

4. Pack Gear Afternoon/Evening

Load bins/bags by category. Stack "first needed" items last (on top or most accessible).
  • Tent + stakes + rainfly in one bag
  • Air mattress + pump + bedding bin
  • Sleeping bags (3)
  • Pillows and blankets
  • Camp chairs (3)
  • Kitchen bin: stove, fuel, pan, pie irons, plates, cups, utensils, cutting board, knife
  • Cooking extras: roasting sticks, skewers, aluminum foil, dish soap, sponge, paper towels, trash bags, ziplock bags
  • Cooler (empty, add ice + food in the morning)
  • Water jug or extra water bottles
  • CamelBak backpack
  • Lantern, headlamps, extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Bug spray, sunscreen
  • Fire starter, lighter/matches (2 sources)
  • Multi-tool, duct tape, paracord
  • Hammock + new straps
  • Life jacket (son)

5. Pack Clothing Evening

Weather: highs 68-71°F, lows 54-55°F. Sunny all 3 days. Layers for cool mornings/evenings.
  • Daytime: t-shirts, shorts, moisture-wicking layers (x3 days each person)
  • Evening: fleece or hoodie for each person
  • Hiking socks (2 pairs per person)
  • Sleepwear / warm pajamas for cool nights
  • Swimsuits (3)
  • Rain jacket (just in case)
  • Sun hats
  • Hiking shoes or sturdy sneakers
  • Water shoes for lake
  • Camp sandals/flip flops
  • Quick-dry towels (swim + bath)

6. Pack Entertainment Evening

  • Print activity book (Activity-Book.docx or activity-book.html)
  • Colored pencils/crayons
  • Card games
  • Books
  • Bluetooth speaker (charged)
  • Binoculars
  • Frisbee or ball
  • Hammock + straps

7. Pack Dog Gear Evening

  • Dog food (pre-portioned bags)
  • Dog bowls (2)
  • Leashes and harnesses
  • Dog towels (for lake swimming)
  • Dog treats
  • Poop bags
  • Familiar blanket for comfort
  • Chew toy or stuffed toy
  • Tie-out / camp tether

8. Hygiene and Personal Evening

  • Toothbrushes, toothpaste
  • Soap, shampoo (biodegradable if possible)
  • Deodorant
  • Toilet paper (backup roll)
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Medications / allergy meds
  • Lip balm with SPF

9. Home Prep for House/Pet Sitter Evening

  • Refresh guest room: clean sheets, clear nightstand, set out towels
  • Clean upstairs bathroom: wipe down, fresh towels, restock toilet paper
  • Leave cash for food/meals
  • Write note with Wi-Fi password
  • Leave emergency contact numbers (vet, neighbor, your cell)
  • Show sitter where pet food, treats, leashes, and poop bags are
  • Write feeding schedule and amounts for any pets staying home
  • Note any medications or special routines for pets
  • Show sitter trash/recycling day and bin locations
  • Leave spare house key in agreed spot
  • Set thermostat / show how HVAC works
  • Point out circuit breaker, water shutoff (just in case)
  • Leave a note about mail/packages
  • Share alarm code or smart lock instructions (if applicable)
  • Let sitter know your return day/time (June 19, ~4:30 PM)

10. Night-Before Final Prep Before bed

  • Charge all phones overnight
  • Download offline maps of Orcas Island / Moran State Park
  • Visit campsite website on phone (caches for offline use)
  • Confirm ferry reservation (if made) or plan to arrive 45 min early
  • Set alarm for 7:30 AM
  • Stage cooler near fridge (pack cold items in the morning)
  • Put car keys, wallet, campsite confirmation by the door
  • Load car with all non-food gear tonight (saves morning stress)

11. Morning of (June 17) 7:30 AM

  • Pack cooler: ice on bottom, frozen items, then cold items on top
  • Add prepped food: tuna salad, pasta salad, eggs, veggies
  • Grab last-minute fridge items: butter, cheese, fruit
  • Final dog walk
  • Lock up house
  • Depart by 8:45 AM for 10:30 AM ferry
