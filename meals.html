Meals & Grocery Game Plan

Simple camp meals for June 17-19, 2026 at Moran State Park

Camp kitchen overview

Crew: 2 adults, 1 child age 6, and 2 dogs. The plan keeps cooking easy, kid-friendly, and flexible with a mix of campfire and camp stove meals.

No red meat Prep-ahead friendly Trail lunch built in

June 16 prep timeline

  • Pre-scramble raw eggs into a sealed jar or container for quick camp breakfast.
  • Make tuna salad, then chill it in a leak-proof container.
  • Make no-bake cookies for an easy trail treat.
  • Wash fruit and carrots, slice peppers, and pack dinner ingredients by meal.
  • Freeze water bottles or ice packs to help the cooler stay cold longer.

Day 1 dinner - First night by the fire

June 17 dinner

Classic campfire hot dogs roasted on sticks, with pasta salad and chips on the side.

Ingredients

  • Hot dogs (turkey or all-beef)
  • Hot dog buns
  • Pasta salad (prep ahead at home)
  • Chips
  • Ketchup, mustard, relish
  • Fruit and veggie snacks (grapes, baby carrots, snap peas)

Directions

  • Roast hot dogs on sticks over the campfire until charred to your liking.
  • Serve in buns with your favorite toppings.
  • Set out pasta salad, chips, and fruit/veggies as sides.

Grilled shrimp kabobs with veggies over the campfire grate. Quick to cook and fun to eat off the skewer.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp (peeled, deveined)
  • Bell peppers, cut into chunks
  • Zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Olive oil, garlic, lemon, salt, pepper
  • Metal or pre-soaked wooden skewers

Directions

  • Thread shrimp and veggies onto skewers, alternating colors.
  • Brush with olive oil and season with garlic, salt, and pepper.
  • Grill over campfire grate 2-3 minutes per side until shrimp are pink.
  • Squeeze lemon over the top before serving.

Day 2 dinner - Easy second-night comfort food

June 18 dinner

Campfire pie iron sandwiches are fun to make and endlessly customizable. Everyone picks their own filling! Pasta salad and chips on the side.

Ingredients

  • Sandwich bread (white or sourdough)
  • Butter for the outside
  • Pizza fillings: marinara, mozzarella, pepperoni (turkey)
  • Dessert fillings: Nutella and banana, or pie filling
  • Savory fillings: ham and cheese, chicken and pesto
  • Pasta salad (prep ahead at home)
  • Chips
  • Fruit and veggie snacks (grapes, baby carrots, snap peas)

Directions

  • Butter the outside of two bread slices.
  • Place one slice butter-side-down in the pie iron, add fillings, top with second slice butter-side-up.
  • Close the iron and cook over campfire coals 3-5 minutes per side until golden and crispy.
  • Let cool slightly before eating - the filling is hot!
  • Set out pasta salad, chips, and fruit/veggies as sides.

Foil packet dinners cook right on the coals. No pots to clean, and everyone can customize their own packet.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • Baby potatoes, halved
  • Broccoli florets
  • Bell peppers, sliced
  • Olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil
  • Pasta salad and chips on the side

Directions

  • Divide chicken and veggies onto large foil squares.
  • Drizzle with olive oil, season generously, and fold into sealed packets.
  • Place on campfire grate or coals for 20-25 minutes, flipping once halfway.
  • Carefully open (steam is hot!) and eat straight from the foil.

Day 3 breakfast - Pancakes and bacon

June 19 breakfast

Classic camp breakfast

Ingredients

  • Pancake mix (just-add-water kind)
  • Bacon
  • Maple syrup
  • Bananas
  • Hot cocoa for everyone

Directions

  • Cook bacon first in the cast iron pan, set aside on a plate.
  • Use the bacon grease to cook pancakes (adds flavor and prevents sticking).
  • Boil water for cocoa on the side burner.
  • Eat, pack camp, and save remaining snacks for the ferry ride home.
