Camp Days by Cascade Lake

Moran State Park, Orcas Island - June 17-19, 2026

Trip rhythm

This plan keeps the days relaxed, kid-friendly, and a little magical. There is plenty of snack time, quiet time, and room to slow down if the first camping adventure needs extra pauses.

Moran State Park Ferry Schedule Cascade Lake Map

Day 1 - June 17: Travel and first campfire

Sunny, high 71°F, low 54°F. West-southwest wind 6-8 mph.
  • 7:30 AM Home
    Wake up, easy breakfast at home, final dog walk, and last cooler check. Aim for calm energy so the 6-year-old starts the trip excited, not rushed.
  • 8:45 AM Travel
    Depart Snohomish. Take US-2 west to I-5 north, then exit 230 to WA-20 west into Anacortes. About 1 hour drive (57 miles). Keep favorite car snacks and a special first-camping playlist handy.
    Leash both dogs before every stop. Give the experienced dog a familiar blanket and the new camper a chew or stuffed toy for comfort.
  • 9:45 AM Travel
    Arrive at Anacortes ferry terminal. Vehicles should be in line 30-45 minutes before departure. Use the restrooms and stretch legs while waiting. Reservations recommended for vehicles.
  • 10:30 AM to 11:30 AM Adventure
    Ferry crossing from Anacortes to Orcas Island (~1 hour). Walk a little, watch for seabirds, and let the first island views build the sense of adventure.
    Family rail-side selfie with the ferry wake behind you, plus a kid-and-dogs portrait with the island coming into view.
    Dogs are allowed only in designated areas and should stay leashed. Choose a quieter corner so the new camper can observe without feeling crowded.
  • 11:30 AM to 1:30 PM Rest
    Easy island drive to Eastsound for lunch (about 20 min from the ferry dock). Browse the village, grab sandwiches, and let everyone decompress before heading to camp.
  • 1:30 PM to 2:30 PM Explore
    Explore the Northend area before check-in. Walk the shoreline, check out the playground, and let the dogs sniff around. No rush; check-in is at 2:30.
    Great time to let both dogs decompress from the car and ferry. Keep them leashed but give them time to sniff and settle in the new environment.
  • 2:30 PM Camp Life
    Check in at Northend Campground, Site 114! Set up camp together. Give the 6-year-old a helper job like placing camp chairs or inflating the air mattress.
    First tent photo, air mattress pump moment, and a "we made it" family shot under the trees.
  • 3:45 PM Hike
    Head out for the short Cascade Falls Trail to stretch travel-weary legs. Bring water, the CamelBak, and a "listen for birds" challenge for the little explorer. The falls are a quick 0.5-mile walk from camp.
    Waterfall backdrop portrait, close-up boot shot near the trail, and family hand-holding photo beneath tall trees.
    Great time to let both dogs decompress from the car and ferry. The cool shady trail helps them settle into the new environment.
  • 5:00 PM Rest
    Back at camp for quiet downtime. Read, color, sip cold drinks, and let everyone adjust to the sounds of camp.
  • 5:30 PM Meal
    First campfire dinner. Use an easy prep-ahead meal so the adults can enjoy the golden hour instead of doing complicated camp cooking.
  • 6:45 PM Campfire
    Campfire story time, hot cocoa or herbal tea, and a simple "best part of the day" circle. Keep the energy soft and wonder-filled.
    As the fire gets brighter and the campground gets noisier, settle the dogs with mats and familiar treats so they can stay close without stress.
  • 8:00 PM Rest
    Pajamas, flashlight fun, and tucked-in tent time. Let the 6-year-old listen to nighttime forest sounds for a few minutes before sleep.
    Lantern glow photo at the tent door, plus a sleepy campfire family portrait.

Day 2 - June 18: Loop trail, swim, and stargazing

Sunny, high 68°F, low 55°F. Light and variable wind, calm evening.
  • 7:00 AM Slow Start
    Wake naturally, snuggle in sleeping bags, and enjoy the fun of the first full morning in camp before getting dressed.
  • 7:30 AM Meal
    Breakfast at camp with prep-ahead scrambled eggs and fruit. Keep cleanup light so the day stays easy.
  • 8:45 AM Hike
    Head out for the Cascade Lake Loop Trail (3 miles, mostly flat). Pack the CamelBak with water and snacks. Go at kid pace, stop often for rocks, moss, and bird calls.
    Park Info
    Keep both dogs leashed and close on narrower sections. Pause before excitement builds too much. Bring poop bags and water for both.
  • 9:00 AM to 11:00 AM Hike
    Explore the loop at kid pace. Turn it into a nature scavenger stroll, stop for photos at lakeside viewpoints, and do only as much as feels fun. No need to finish the full loop if legs get tired.
    Lakeside family portrait, close-up boot shot on the trail, and a candid of the 6-year-old discovering something cool.
  • 11:15 AM Snack
    Post-hike snack at camp. Rehydrate and rest before the afternoon swim. Let everyone decompress so nobody crashes early.
  • 12:00 PM Meal
    Lunch at camp with tuna salad and simple sides. After lunch, switch the whole camp into low gear.
  • 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM Quiet Time
    True afternoon quiet time. Read in camp chairs, let the 6-year-old draw in the activity book, and give the dogs a solid nap in shade. This rest block protects the happy evening energy.
    This is the best time for a reset walk, water refill, and calm praise for the new camper. Keep camp extra peaceful.
  • 2:45 PM Swim
    Head to the Cascade Lake sandy beach for swim time and shoreline play. Keep it unhurried with plenty of warming-up breaks since there is no lifeguard.
    Wet-haired beach grin, toes-in-sand family lineup, and a lakeside candid while skipping rocks.
  • 4:00 PM Rest
    Dry off, change into warm layers, and have a snack back at camp. Everyone gets a quiet reset before dinner.
  • 5:45 PM Meal
    Easy camp dinner with a stove or fire option, followed by no-bake cookies for dessert. Let the 6-year-old help with one simple dinner job for camping confidence.
  • 7:00 PM Games
    Campfire card games, flashlight shadow play, and a mini family awards moment for "brave hiker," "best lake splash," and "camp dog patrol."
  • 8:30 PM Stargazing
    Because the evening should be calm and clear, finish with blanket-wrapped stargazing. Point out the brightest stars and let the 6-year-old invent constellations.
    Lantern-lit family silhouette and a blanket cuddle photo before full dark.
    If either dog seems done with the evening, one adult can do a quick final leash walk while the other stays with the sleepy kiddo.

Day 3 - June 19: Last lake visit and ferry home

Sunny, high 71°F. Bright morning for pack-up and a smooth return.
  • 7:00 AM Slow Start
    Wake up gently and enjoy the final morning light in camp. Keep bags mostly packed before breakfast so nobody feels rushed later.
  • 7:45 AM Meal
    Simple breakfast and hot drinks. Talk about favorite moments from the trip while the camp still feels cozy.
  • 8:30 AM Pack Up
    Begin breaking camp in stages: bedding first, then loose gear, then kitchen items. Give the 6-year-old a "camp closing crew" role with a tiny checklist.
  • 9:45 AM Lake Visit
    Take one last mellow walk to Cascade Lake for shoreline play, a goodbye look at the water, and a final deep breath of forest air.
    Final family photo at the lake, plus one sweet portrait of the kiddo with both dogs near the trail.
  • 10:30 AM Rest
    Back to camp for a snack, bathroom break, and a low-key finish to packing. This buffer keeps the departure calm.
    Offer water and a quick sniff walk before the car ride. A calm last hour helps the new camping dog end on a good note.
  • 11:30 AM Travel
    Final campsite sweep, load the car, and check out. Drive toward Eastsound (about 15 min from Moran).
  • 12:00 PM to 1:30 PM Explore
    Visit the fort in Eastsound and explore the village one last time. Grab ice cream as a farewell treat. Let the 6-year-old run around and burn off energy before the ferry ride.
    Fort exploration photos, ice cream smiles, and a last village stroll portrait.
  • 1:45 PM Travel
    Drive to the Orcas ferry terminal (about 10 min from Eastsound). Line up for the 2:20 PM sailing home, with the 5:25 PM departure as a backup cushion.
  • 2:20 PM to 3:20 PM Travel
    Ferry ride back from Orcas to Anacortes. Settle in with lunch snacks, quiet coloring, and a gentle recap of the trip.
  • 3:30 PM to 4:30 PM Home Stretch
    Drive home to Snohomish via WA-20 east to I-5 south to US-2 east (~1 hour). Keep the rest of the evening light so the adventure can end sweetly instead of in a rush.
