Easy Adventures for First Camp Memories

Mellow to moderate fun for June 17-19, 2026 at Moran State Park

Trip Rhythm

This plan keeps the pace gentle for a first-ever camping trip. Think short hikes, plenty of snack breaks, beach time, campsite play, and flexible adventures that can end early if anyone is tired.

Cascade Falls Trail Hike

Distance: About 0.5 miles one way Terrain: Forest trail, some steps near the falls Duration: 30-45 minutes round trip Bring: Water, snacks, camera Dog-friendly: Yes, on leash

A short, rewarding forest walk with a big payoff at the waterfall. This is an ideal first hike because it feels adventurous without asking too much from a 6-year-old or the dogs.

Kid tip: Let him lead the way and count different kinds of moss and ferns along the trail.

Waterfall viewpoint, mossy bridges, misty forest frames.
Leash up before the steps near the falls so both dogs stay steady on the narrower sections.

Trail details

Cascade Lake Loop Trail

Distance: About 3 miles Terrain: Mostly flat lakeside path Duration: 1.5-2 hours at kid pace Bring: Water, snacks, swimsuits Dog-friendly: Yes, on leash

A mellow loop with lake views, bridges, and plenty to notice along the way. You can stop at the beach midway, turn it into a half-loop adventure, or bail out early if the first camping trip energy starts to dip.

Kid tip: Bring the activity book and check off scavenger hunt finds as you go.

Lake reflections, old growth trees, wooden bridges, family trail moments.
This is a great sniff walk. Plan extra water stops for both dogs, especially if the afternoon warms up.

Swimming at Cascade Lake

Location: Sandy beach at the day-use area Water: Cool but swimmable, about 60-65°F in June Duration: Stay as long as it feels fun Bring: Swimsuits, towels, water shoes, sunscreen, snacks Dog-friendly: Dogs can swim in non-designated swim areas

A perfect midday reset. Beach play gives your first-time camper freedom to splash, dig, and explore without the structure of a formal activity.

Safety: No lifeguard on duty, supervise closely near the water and dock.

Kid tip: Build sand structures, skip rocks, and look for tadpoles near the shoreline.

Splash shots, dock photos, mountain-and-lake views, sandy toes at the shoreline.
If the dogs swim, choose a quieter shoreline spot away from the main swim zone.

Campfire Evening

Setup: Buy firewood at the park, or bring your own Duration: After dinner until bedtime Bring: Roasting sticks, marshmallows, card games, star chart or app

Keep the first campfire simple and cozy. Toast s'mores, trade short stories, play easy card games by lantern light, and let bedtime happen before anyone gets overtired.

Campfire ideas: S'mores, silly stories, quiet card games, constellation spotting.

Golden hour at camp, lantern glow, silhouette shots by the fire ring.
Keep both dogs on lead and away from the fire. The nervous dog may prefer the tent during campfire time.

Nature Exploration

When: Throughout the trip Energy: Flexible, low-pressure discovery Bring: Activity book, paper, crayons, small day bag

Use these as mini-adventures whenever you have a quiet pocket of time:

  • Rock flipping for bugs and salamanders, then carefully put rocks back
  • Birdwatching for bald eagles, herons, and woodpeckers
  • Leaf and bark rubbings with paper and crayons
  • Collecting pinecones and interesting sticks
  • Listening for frogs at the lake around dusk
  • Watching for deer, which are very common in the park
  • Working through the activity book scavenger hunt
Tiny nature finds in little hands, deer sightings, bark rubbing masterpieces.

Campsite Fun

When: Morning downtime, post-hike breaks, pre-dinner hours Energy: Easy and playful

Not every memorable moment has to be a destination. The campsite can do a lot of the magic.

  • Card games like Go Fish, Uno, and War
  • Bluetooth speaker music during camp chores
  • Frisbee or ball play in open areas
  • Hammock time, if you brought one
  • Reading books together in camp chairs
  • Coloring and drawing in the activity book
Hammock lounging, card table giggles, campsite reading nook moments.
A tie-out can give the dogs a little range while you cook or set up games, while still following camp rules.

Stargazing

Best time: 9:30-10 PM Sunset: About 9:15 PM in mid-June Bring: Blanket, stargazing app, warm layer

Moran State Park has minimal light pollution, so the stars can be excellent on a clear night. Keep it simple for a 6-year-old by starting with one recognizable shape and building from there.

What to look for: Big Dipper, North Star, and any visible June planets.

Kid tip: Start with just the Big Dipper, then follow it to the North Star.

Blanket-on-the-ground sky shots, flashlight-lit faces, twilight tree silhouettes.

Tips for Camping with Dogs

  • Keep both dogs leashed at all times. That is the park rule.
  • Bring familiar blankets or beds so each dog has a comfortable place to settle.
  • Stick to their normal feeding schedule to keep the routine familiar.
  • The nervous dog may do better in the tent during campfire time.
  • Watch for wildlife encounters, especially deer, which are very common here.
  • Bring extra water for both dogs on hikes and during lake play.
  • Bring poop bags and pack it out.
  • Consider a tie-out at camp so they have some range while you cook.
