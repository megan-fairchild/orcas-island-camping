Day-by-Day Clothing Guide

Day 1 - Travel and camp setup

71°F / 54°F: Warm in the afternoon, cooler once the sun drops.

Wear comfortable travel clothes that can go straight into setup mode. T-shirts, shorts, or lightweight pants work well for the ferry, unloading, and getting camp organized.

  • Start with a breathable t-shirt or moisture-wicking top.
  • Choose shorts for the day, but keep long pants easy to grab for evening.
  • Bring a fleece or hoodie in the top of the duffel for sunset and campsite wind.
  • Use sturdy sneakers or hiking shoes for hauling gear around camp.

Day 2 - Adventure day

68°F / 55°F: Sunny hiking weather, then a calm, cooler evening.

Plan for a full active day with hiking, swimming, and campfire time. Quick-dry layers help everyone stay comfortable if the lake is part of the plan.

  • Hiking outfit: moisture-wicking top, shorts, hiking socks, and sturdy shoes.
  • Pack swimsuits, quick-dry towels, and water shoes for the lake.
  • Keep sun hats on hand for the long June daylight.
  • Switch to long pants, a fleece or hoodie, and dry socks for campfire time.

Day 3 - Pack up and travel home

71°F daytime: Easy, comfortable clothes make break-down simpler.

Choose clothes that can handle a little dirt while packing camp, then stay comfortable for the ferry and drive home.

  • Fresh t-shirt and shorts or light pants for a cleaner ride home.
  • Closed-toe shoes help with loading bins, stakes, and wet gear.
  • Keep one light layer out for the morning cool-down.
  • Set aside a small bag for damp swimsuits, towels, or dog gear.

Nighttime - Warm sleep layers

54-55°F nights: The air mattress will feel colder than the air temperature.

Night comfort matters most on this trip. The air mattress can pull heat away from you from below, even when the forecast looks mild.

  • Wear warm pajamas and thick socks for sleeping.
  • Use sleeping bags or layered sheets and warm blankets that are comfortable around 50°F nights.
  • Place a foam pad, thick blanket, or emergency blanket under the sleeper or between the mattress and bedding for insulation.
  • Keep an extra blanket within reach for the child and one for early morning lounging.
